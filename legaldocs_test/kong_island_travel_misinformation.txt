Case No. 2025‑TOUR‑778

Tourism Bureau v. “Adventurist Travel Guides, Ltd.”

Administrative Fine – Misrepresentation of Extreme Environments

OFFICE OF GLOBAL HAZARDOUS DESTINATIONS REGULATION

Facts

The Respondent published a travel pamphlet describing expeditions to Kong Skull Island as:

“A walk in the park.”

Respondent failed to clarify that the “park” in question:

Contained an ecosystem consistent with “Jurassic Park–class hazards,”

Featured megafauna including apes exceeding 150 feet in height, predatory lizards, and airborne threats of unspecified genus.

At least three tourists, relying on this description, arrived with:

Standard running shoes,

Snack bars,

Enthusiasm unwarranted for environments requiring armored transport.

Sanction

Respondent is hereby fined:

4,000 credits,

Obligation to print updated brochures with appropriate warnings, including but not limited to,
“May require fleeing from creatures not evaluated by modern paleontology.”

Filed and executed this 25th day of November, 2025
